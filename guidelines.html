EIR Rating Guidelines

See exactly how we score packaging from 1–5 stars using our Visibility + Pressure + Progress framework.

EIR Packaging Guidelines

High EIR (4–5★)

Key Features:

  • ✔ Fully recyclable in yellow‐bin
  • ✔ Mono‐materials (e.g. PP or PET)
  • ✔ >30% post‐consumer recycled content
  • ✔ Certified compostable

Impact:

  • • Low carbon footprint
  • • Diverts waste from landfill
  • • Supports closed‐loop systems

Examples:

rPET Water Bottle
Compostable Mailer
Mono‐material Yoghurt Tub
BioPak Sugarcane Tray
Moderate EIR (2–3★)

Key Features:

  • ✔ Partially/conditionally recyclable
  • ✔ Small material mixes
  • ✔ 10–29% recycled content
  • ✔ Unverified compost claims

Impact:

  • • Sorting challenges
  • • Potential contamination

Examples:

Mixed‐Material Tray
Alu‐Plastic Pouch
“Biodegradable” Wrapper
Low EIR (0–1★)

Key Features:

  • ✖ Non‐recyclable plastics (e.g. PVC)
  • ✖ Multi‐layer that can’t be separated
  • ✖ 0% recycled content
  • ✖ Not compostable

Impact:

  • • High landfill/ocean pollution
  • • Lost recovery opportunities

Examples:

Foil‐Plastic Chip Bag
Polystyrene Foam
Mixed Snack Wrapper

Local Recycling & Proper Disposal

Find tailored recycling instructions for your council and expert tips to make sure your packaging ends up in the right place.

Please select your council to view precise recycling guidelines.
General Disposal Tips
  • ✔ Rinse containers and remove caps before recycling.
  • ✔ Flatten cardboard and paperboard to save space.
  • ✔ Keep mixed materials (e.g. foil-plastic pouches) separate—check your council’s policy.
  • ✔ Compost certified compostable items only in industrial composting facilities where accepted.
  • ✔ When in doubt, consult your local council’s website or call them for specific queries.
